About

Hello,

Hello, I'm Chirag C. Shetty or simply Chitty if you can't roll the 'r'.

I am an Engineer. I like to learn how things work. I love to build stuff. At the same time I try to get the big picture. Technology continues to transform our lives, businesses and society at large. I aspire to work at the intersection of development and deployment of such technology.

Robert Noyce and David Packard - both brilliant engineers and visionary business leaders - are my role models among many others. As a researcher, I greatly admire Claude Shannon (he quantified something as abstract as 'information'. How cool is that!).

I graduated from IIT Bombay in 2017 with Dual Degrees (Bachelors & Masters) in Electrical Engineering. I specialized in Communication Systems & Signal Processing. I was awarded the President Dr. Shankar Dayal Sharma Gold Medal at IIT-B Convocation 2017.

I have worked at Texas Instruments, Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC) and Deutsche Bank in the past. I have 3 published research works and 2 patents (more under review). Since January 2020 I am a graduate researcher at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC)

2020 - Current

At UIUC - 5G, IoT, AI/ML

At UIUC, I am exploring topics in Wireless Networks, Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Distributed Systems & Computer Security. 2016-17

Indian Regional Navigation System (IRNSS) Reciever Design

IIT-Bombay

When India completed the 7 satellite IRNSS in April 2016, I decided to build a receiver. I did it as my Master's Thesis with Prof. Madhav Desai, and built this. IRNSS, is a navigation system like the GPS (owned by the USA), except just for the Indian Subcontinent. I was amazed at the engineering behind GPS. By studying GPS, we built a similar reciever stack for IRNSS. We built an end-to-end system complete with an analog front end and demonstrated by calculating ranges to the satellities. This work won the Undergraduate Research Award (URA03).

2017-2019

Information Theory

IIT Bombay

I am amused how communication over these invisible waves work? Wireless communication seems magical. I was lucky to find a mentor in Prof. Sibiraj Pillai who introduced me to wide variety of concepts in the field and in particular to Information Theory. We started to work on decentralized MIMO MAC Channel in 2017, which finally culminated in us obtaining close bounds for capacity of such channels. I worked on it at nights while working at Texas Instruemnts. This first-author work was published at ISIT 2019, Paris. [link]

2014-2015

Bio-Inspired Computing

IIT Bombay

At IITB I was fortunate to work with Prof. Bipin Rajendran, and be introduced to the intriguing world of bio-computing. We built this charming bot that is controlled by 10 microcontoller neurons. This first-author work was demonstrated at ISCAS 2015, Lisbon [link]. I believe extracting the working principles of biological computing, just like we extracted the working principles of flight a century ago will be a defining problem of this century (I picked the analogy from Prof. Rajendran)

Professional

Bangalore, India

Was part of the high-speed converters (HSC) team. Worked on data-converters for wireless infrastructure market (5G MIMO & small cell) under amazing guidance of TI-Fellow Vishy Pentakota. Joined as third member of a team building a very novel state-of-the-art analog-to-digital converter. In a fast-paced 18 months, we took an idea from conception to design to silicon tapeout.
