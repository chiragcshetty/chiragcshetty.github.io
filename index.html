Hello

Hello, I'm Chirag. I graduated from IIT Bombay in 2017 with Bachelor's and Master's Degree in Electrical Engineering.

At IITB I was fortunate to work with Prof. Bipin Rajendran, and be introduced to the intriguing world of bio-computing. We built this charming bot that is controlled by 10 microcontoller neurons. I believe extracting the working principles of biological computing, just like we extracted the working principles of flight a century ago will be a defining problem of this century (I picked the analogy from Prof. Rajendran)

I am equally amused how communication over these invisible waves work? Wireless communication seems magical. I was lucky to find a mentor in Prof. Sibiraj Pillai who introduced me to wide variety of concepts in the field and in particular to Information Theory. When India completed the 7 satellite navigation system (IRNSS), we decided to build a receiver. To do so I did it as my Master's Thesis with Prof. Madhav Desai, and built this.

Then I was briefly at TSMC, Taiwan, and am currently happily working at Texas Instruments as analog design engineer mentored by Vishy Pentakota

During my time at IITB I was the Overall Coordinator of Student Technical Activities Body (STAB) and lead the Tinkerers' Lab of IIT Bombay.
